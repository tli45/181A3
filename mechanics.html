How Does Ollie Work?

Understand the physics and motion behind this iconic move.

Dynamics Breakdown

Ollie consists of three different motion systems: the jumping motion of the rider and the rotating motion of the board. In other words, ollie is simply the combined motion of jumping and kicking the board.

The jumping motion system consists solely of the rider, who jumps on the board. Simply put, what a rider does in an ollie is to jump on a moving board. A detailed analysis showed that the rider exerts the jumping force onto the board, which propagates this force to the ground, resulting in a reaction force exerted on the rider. This causes the rider (mass body) to move upwards with an initial force roughly equal to the jumping force in magnitude.

The rotating motion of the board is caused by the kicking force (jumping force); the wheels act as a rotating axis and support, resulting in a moment at the axis that lifts the front end of the board. This motion occurs rapidly, which, due to inertia, causes the board to decelerate. The center of mass is located in the middle of the board, and similar motions will result in a straight-line velocity, flying in the tangent direction to the last point of the rotating curve.

Lastly, when the back foot hits the board, the rider and the board are at its maximum height.
